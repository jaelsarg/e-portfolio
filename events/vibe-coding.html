Vibe Coding

📍 Howest Brugge, Belgium
📅 10/03/2026 - 08:00-18:00
🎤 Person

This hackathon, organized by Howest, took place from 9:00 to 18:00 and brought together IT and Business students to collaborate on innovative solutions. The event focused on “vibe coding,” encouraging participants to create applications using low-code and no-code tools.

Participants could choose between three different tracks: The Paperwork Killer (B2B), The Hyper-Local Hero (consumer), and The Data Storyteller (analytics). Each team selected a track and worked on developing an application that addressed the chosen problem. At the end of the day, all teams presented their solutions to a panel of lecturers.

An interesting aspect of the event was the presence of international lecturers. They offered different perspectives and insights, which added an extra layer of learning and inspiration throughout the day.

During the hackathon, I learned how to build a functional website without writing traditional code by using Lovable, an AI-powered tool. While this was my first time working with Lovable specifically, I had prior exposure to low-code and no-code approaches during an international project in Poland. This experience helped me quickly adapt and make the most of the tool.

What made this event particularly valuable was the collaboration between IT and Business students. Working with people from different backgrounds provided new insights, especially in understanding how business perspectives influence technical solutions. It also strengthened teamwork and communication skills in a multidisciplinary environment.

The event was well organized, and I do not have specific suggestions for improvement. Overall, it was both educational and enjoyable.

I would definitely consider participating again and would recommend this hackathon to others. It is a great opportunity to learn new tools, gain practical experience, and collaborate with people from different fields.
